Earthdawn Players Compendium FAQ
Last Updated By Dammi on 2/9/06 		This applies only to the first version of the eBook (August 2005)!
Second Weapon Errata (Talents; p. 199)
Answer: Description should read: The Second Weapon talent allows an adept to attack with two different melee weapons in the same round. Both weapons must be able to be wielded with one hand. The second weapon is held in the off-hand and must be at least one Size smaller than the adept’s primary weapon. The adept makes a Second Weapon Test as a bonus melee Attack Test. If the test succeeds, the adept makes a Damage Test for the weapon, as normal. T’skrang characters may use a tail weapon as a second weapon.
Question:
Earthdawn Players Compendium FAQ
Last Updated By Dammi on 2/9/06 		The additional damage effect from Lizard Leap is gone. Why did you downgrade this talent?
Answer: Part of the problem with the Beastmaster was that he was really more combat-blender than animal-handler
in ED1. The old Lizard Leap version simply contributed to this, and (speaking from a broader point of view) copied the effects of Down Strike. Combat-loving Beastmasters can still get this effect by learning the Down Strike skill (or talent via Versatility).

The following applies only to the first version of the eBook (August 2005):
However, we spotted two errata while answering ths question. First, a passage of text is missing from the Lizard Leap talent:

(...) Lizard Leap can also be used to avoid environmental hazards, such as pit traps and falling rocks. If the adept is aware of the threat, he may make a Lizard Leap Test against a Difficulty Number determined by the gamemaster to avoid being struck. If the test succeeds, the adept manages to leap clear at the last instant. Lizard Leap can only be used to avoid environmental hazards, it cannot be used in place of a normal Avoid Blow Test.

Second, the Beastmaster’s talent progression got messed up - Lizard Leap is supposed to be available at Third Circle, not Sixth. The entries should read:

Third Circle Talents: Lizard Leap (D), Cat’s Paw (D)
Fifth Circle Talents: Borrow Sense (D), Heal Animal Servant (D), Ritual of the Ghost
Master (D)
Sixth Circle Talents: Endure Cold, Incite Stampede (D)

Second Riposte Errata (Talent Knacks; p. 226)
Answer: Rank: 7
Description should read: The Second Riposte talent knack allows an adept to attempt another riposte against the same melee attack. The adept must be wielding two one-handed weapons. Question:
Earthdawn Players Compendium FAQ
Last Updated By Dammi on 2/9/06 		Page 422 says that in order for an adept to learn a talent from a higher circle he has to train 80 hours in a four weeks period. Then in the example it says its a 2 weeks period. Whats right?
Answer: Both are: the adept in the example happens to be doing his 80 hours of training in the space of two weeks-8 hours a day for ten days (think of your typical 5-day work week... that’s kind of what he’s doing). Since 2 weeks is less than the 4-week time limit, the training works. The adept could just as easily do 3 days one week, 2 days the next, 2 days the next, and 3 the last week (again, assuming an 8-hour training day). As long as a total of 80 hours of training are fit into a 4 week period, that’s fine.
