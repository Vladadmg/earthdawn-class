Links

We would love to add a link to your Earthdawn web site. To do this, simply drop us an email at info@earthdawn.com. Please make sure you include a short introduction to your site, describing what sort of information you have supporting Earthdawn!

ardanyan.de
An Earthdawn site hosting a number of inofficial supplements and character sheets in the german and english languages. Not all supplements are available in both languages, although some are in the process of being translated.

Earthdawn and the 7 Sands
An Earthdawn website featuring all Earthdawn disciplines -- and even some new ones -- as well as a unique animal training system, animal skill system, skill system, and BattleDAWN 2.0.

E-Dawn Discussion Group
E-Dawn e-zine archive location, as well as discussion group/forum for information about the Earthdawn and the 7 Sands Homepage in particular and Earthdawn in general.

Lost Papers EDCG
Home of the ancient EDCG 1.3e character generator. Soon to become home of the new version of EDCG 2.0. During development a forum will be accessible to share ideas and critics.

EDPT
The Earthdawn Publishing Trust. An excellent source for many things Earthdawn from fans around the world. Also home for the Book of Tomorrow, a semi-regular fanzine.

RedBrick Limited
Our company homepage, containing general information about us and a complete listing of our products.

Runners-Heaven Earthdawn
A German web site providing a Forum (Chartalk, Inplays and Rule Clarifications/Houserules), Descriptions of the playable Races and Disciplines, Reviews of different ED Products, and a small but steadily growing Download Area for Supplements made by ED-Fans.

StiggyBaby
Stiggybaby’s Online Discount Gaming Store has an ever-growing selection of roleplaying games, and specializes in Earthdawn. Stiggybaby’s is located in the United States, and ships worldwide. They have stocks of out-of-print Earthdawn books, so check them out!

The Ancient Files
The Ancient Files includes a Sperethiel Dictionary, a list of Earthdawn/Shadowrun crossovers, and a whole lot more.

The Lore Merchant
The personal website of Josh Harrison, it contains many campaign journals from past Earthdawn games, a few articles and GM support tools (including the original incarnation of the Earthdawn Timeline), and other items of interest to fans of role-playing games, fiction, and fantasy.
