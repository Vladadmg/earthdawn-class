NAME-GIVER’S COMPENDIUM: OPEN CALL FOR WRITERS

For the forthcoming Name-givers Compendium, we intend to revise and expand the racial Disciplines—covering all of them in more detail and with better mechanics. While the game mechanics are currently in development, we need some in-game fiction explaining each Discipline’s concepts and unique views.

Since this is an open call, everyone can participate. Please read the following guidelines very carefully before submitting. Submissions that do not conform to the guidelines will not be accepted.

WHAT WE ARE LOOKING FOR
Fictional accounts written from the point of view of followers of the Disciplines in question (a list of Disciplines is provided below). The tone and style of the text must match those in the Player’s Compendium (previously published in FASA’s Adept’s Way).

We’re using this competition as hunting ground to find potential new writers we can send outlines to and ask to participate in bigger projects, so succeeding here may result in future agreements.

Formatting
•    We accept electronic submissions only. Each entry should come as a separate file (please use either of the following formats for your entries: DOC, RTF, TXT, and PDF).
•    Your full name and eMail-address should be provided on all pages of each entry.
•    The minimum length of each fictional entry is 1/3 of a page, maximum length is 2 pages (a page being defined as one side of plain white letter-sized or A4 paper with one-inch margins all around, and set in type no smaller than 10-point).
•    Pay close attention to spelling, grammar, and punctuation—using our Submission Handbook for Earthdawn as guideline.

Disciplines
•    Boatman (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. I)
•    Horror Stalker (Horrors)
•    Journeyman (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. I)
•    Liberator (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. II)
•    Messenger (new Discipline)
•    Outcast Warrior (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. II)
•    Purifier (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. II)
•    Shaman (Magic: A Manual of Mystic Secrets)
•    Taildancer (Earthdawn Journal, Vol. VI)
•    Traveled Scholar (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. II)
•    Wind-Dancer (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. I)
•    Windmaster (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. I)
•    Windscout (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. I)
•    Woodsman (Denizens of Earthdawn, Vol. I)

FAQ
If you have any question in addition to the below, use our Forums or eMail us at info@earthdawn.com.

Can I have a look the revised game mechanics?
No. While all Disciplines will be developed up to Fifteenth Circle, most of the Disciplines listed above will be customized variants of existing Discipline, so you can get a rough idea of what the mechanics and concepts will be like by looking at those. Here’s a list of which Disciplines are compareable: Boatman—Air Sailor, Messenger—Scout, Outcast Warrior—Warrior, Purifier—Warrior, Wind-Dancer—Illusionist/Troubadour, Windmaster—Warrior, Windscout—Scout, Woodsman—Scout/Beastmaster.
It might be interesting to know that the revised version of the Shaman will be able to summon spirits of nature (which is a new type of spirit that will be introduced in the book).

What kind of Discipline is the Messenger?
Messengers are a variant of the Scout Discipline, and act as reliable couriers. Obsidimen are the only race that provides the necessary single-mindedness to deliver a message at all costs, and is the only race that excels at this Discipline.

I don’t have the Earthdawn Journal to look up the Taildancer!
The Taildancer is a racial Discipline for the t’skrang, and can be found here. Our version of this Discipline won’t be specific to the winged t’skrang, however.
