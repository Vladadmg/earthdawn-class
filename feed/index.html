Sometimes bad reception on a cell phone can be due to the service provider having no antennae or boosters in your area. This is usually the case in rural areas where they believe that the relatively few users in that area, makes it financially viable to spend money to improve the services reception. This though, can also be a problem in some urban areas but is for a difference reason. In urban areas there certainly are enough people around using cell phone services to warrant placement of antennae but, due to signals weakening on passing through buildings, sometimes areas as big as a few blocks, can experience bad reception. This is more often the case where there are older buildings like churches which, at the time of their building, a lot of metal including lead were used in the building materials.

 

 

Therefore if you live, work or even just visit one of these areas where the reception is bad, you too may want to consider using a cell phone booster. If you do you will have to decide what kind of booster you want as there are some kinds that are ideal for vehicles, particularly handy for motorhomes or campers, some that are specifically designed for desk use but can also be used in small apartments and then there are those kinds which are designed to be used in larger buildings or in the centre of malls which are notorious for having bad reception areas. Although all kinds are available, there are only two major types, the smart signal booster and the analog booster. Although the analog boosters can and often are effective, they do have exterior directional antennae which will probably need professional help to set up. The smart signal boosters are a plug in and go device as they possess internal, multi-directional antennae. Whichever you decide to buy, you can always get signal booster support but before you buy, you should perhaps look at the laws and regulations that exist in your country, concerning the use of these boosters.

 
In the UK it is illegal to use a booster without a license and a license can only be obtained after the service provider has declared that they cannot improve the service in that particular area. Once a license has been obtained, only boosters that meet all EU criteria are allowed to be used.
In Australia, as it is considered that these boosters may interfere with other people’s receptions, they are only allowed to be bought from their service provider and they must keep strict records as to who bought them and where they are being used.

 
In the United States, as long as the booster complies with FCC regulations, boosters may be used however; those regulations received large updates in 2013 and so ensure your chosen device complies with those updates.
